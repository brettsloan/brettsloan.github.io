Scripts:

Debit Script

You, (Client’s name), authorize for a single debit entry to your debit card ending in **** through BluePay to make a onetime payment in the amount of $$ to Rifco National Auto Finance account # (LC Contract ID). Your total payment of $$ will be dated on DD/MM/YYYY. To authorize this payment, please state your first and last name.

Payout Request Script

Please be advised that the following provided amount is subject to change. Providing all past payments clear and allowing for any additional fees associated with returned payment. The balance due as of today’s day PAYOUT (30) includes 30 days of per diem at PER DIEM. If there is a discrepancy between this amount verbally provided and an amount provided in writing, the amount in writing will be the honored amount. Please acknowledge this statement by stating your first and last name.

Frequency Change Script

You are giving Rifco permission to charge a fee of $75 to process the requested frequency change. This fee will be interest free and will be added to the end of your contract. Do you acknowledge this adjustment?

Representment Campaign

  • We tried to pull your scheduled payment from your account and there was insufficient funds in the account.
  • We will be attempting to pull that payment again. Are the funds there now?
  • What is going on that the funds weren’t available?
  • When can you make up the payment? (If the funds won’t be there for the representment.)

Reference Campaign

  • I was given your name and telephone on a credit application for (client)
  • *Once confirmed state Outbound Disclosure (This call may be monitored...)*
  • Is this a friend or relative of yours?
  • I am calling today to verify place of employment, home address and contact phone numbers. Do you know where (the client) is currently working? A home address? What number do you use to contact (the client)?
  • Would you be able to pass a message to (the client) for me?

Employer Campaign

  • I am calling to complete an employment verification. Who should I be speaking to?
  • Can you verify that (the client) is still one of your employees?
  • Do you have multiple business locations or just this one? What is the address of the location (the client) works at?
  • Is there a designated employee parking lot at that location? Is it accessible to the public or secured?
  • Is (the client) at work today? Would it be possible to speak to him/her?
  • Does he/she work shift work? What are the regular operating hours?
  • If I can’t speak to (the client), can I leave an urgent message for him/her?

DNC Third Party Numbers

  • If the Third party asks us not to call again, please send an email to your Supervisor with the number and the reason it needs to be DNC’d.
  • The Supervisor will tick the DNC box if the Supervisor concurs.
  • If a new employer is located, the old employer information can be removed and replaced with the new info.

Subsequent Third Party Calls

  • If we speak to an employer or a reference a 2nd or 3rd time, ensure we acknowledge the first conversation:
    1. Is the client still an employee? Still working at the same business address? Same shift? Can I speak to him/her now?
    2. Does the reference have any new contact information, new address or new contact information? Was the reference successful in passing along a message the last time we spoke?
