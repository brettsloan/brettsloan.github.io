Quality Score Card:

Call Open

* Did the Agent correctly disclose the Outbound Call Recording Disclosure Statement?

 * Were the proper points of customer verification completed?

Collection Core Skills

 * Did the Agent identify and discuss the Reason for Delinquency?

  Did the Agent discuss and/or challenge account history, including payment history details within the conversation?
  (IE: Broken promises, NSF's, difficult contact, abnormal RFD patterns, potentially compromised collateral or customer info)

Responses / Rebuttals

  Did the Agent use appropriate responses to all client objections?

Promise To Pay / Payment

  Did the Agent outline the total amount due & make arrangements for the full arrears balance?

* Did the Agent negotiate to obtain payment arrangements and determine what could be paid today?

  Did the Agent discuss Source of Funds and/or employment information to determine PTP validity?

  Did the Agent address/discuss the the next regular payment due?

  Did the Agent discuss the benefits and possible consequences of payment/non-payment to create urgency?

* Did the Agent recite the Mini-Miranda?
(Prior to processing a Blue Pay - Visa Debit/Master card payment)

Professionalism

  Did the Agent project courtesy and professionalism?
  (IE: Strong product and client knowledge, proper tone, pace, control, word usage, etc.)

Third Party Contact / Messages

  Were appropriate third party guidelines followed?
  (Reference, employer, DNC'd third parties)

Call Wrap Up

  Did the Agent or client recap all payment dates?

  Did the Agent or client recap all payment amounts?

  Does the agent's note and actioned statuses reflect how the call went and include all pertinent details?
  (Including the update of all demographics)

* Did the Agent disposition the call properly through LiveVox?

  Did the Agent set a proper follow-up date?

* If this criteria is missed, the agent scores zero for the entire category

Representment Campaign

  • We tried to pull your scheduled payment from your account and there was insufficient funds in the account.
  • We will be attempting to pull that payment again. Are the funds there now?
  • What is going on that the funds weren’t available?
  • When can you make up the payment? (If the funds won’t be there for the representment.)

Reference Campaign

  • I was given your name and telephone on a credit application for (client)
  • *Once confirmed state Outbound Disclosure (This call may be monitored...)*
  • Is this a friend or relative of yours?
  • I am calling today to verify place of employment, home address and contact phone numbers. Do you know where (the client) is currently working? A home address? What number do you use to contact (the client)?
  • Would you be able to pass a message to (the client) for me?

Employer Campaign

  • I am calling to complete an employment verification. Who should I be speaking to?
  • Can you verify that (the client) is still one of your employees?
  • Do you have multiple business locations or just this one? What is the address of the location (the client) works at?
  • Is there a designated employee parking lot at that location? Is it accessible to the public or secured?
  • Is (the client) at work today? Would it be possible to speak to him/her?
  • Does he/she work shift work? What are the regular operating hours?
  • If I can’t speak to (the client), can I leave an urgent message for him/her?

DNC Third Party Numbers

  • If the Third party asks us not to call again, please send an email to your Supervisor with the number and the reason it needs to be DNC’d.
  • The Supervisor will tick the DNC box if the Supervisor concurs.
  • If a new employer is located, the old employer information can be removed and replaced with the new info.

Subsequent Third Party Calls

  • If we speak to an employer or a reference a 2nd or 3rd time, ensure we acknowledge the first conversation:
    1. Is the client still an employee? Still working at the same business address? Same shift? Can I speak to him/her now?
    2. Does the reference have any new contact information, new address or new contact information? Was the reference successful in passing along a message the last time we spoke?

Frequency Change Formulas

  • Monthly - BW: 12*(payment)/26
  • Monthly - SM: (payment)/2
  • BW - Monthly: 26*(payment)/12
  • BW - SM: 26*(payment)/24
  • SM - Monthly: (payment)*2
  • SM - BW: 24*(payment)/26

Frequency Change & Due Date Change Guidelines:

Due Date Change (DDC):

The process of permanently moving a payment schedule forward or backwards a set number of days while maintaining the same frequency.

Frequency Change (FC):

The process of permanently changing the frequency of a payment schedule (biweekly, monthly, or semi-monthly payment schedule).

DDC Rules/Guidelines:

  • No administration fee is charged.
  • The future requested payment date cannot go past the client’s next or previous amortized due date (the original schedule). In other words, the client cannot move their schedule more than a billing period ahead.

FC Rules/Guidelines:

  • Account must be up to date.
  • In order to finalize the FC, the first payment on the new schedule must clear. If it does not, the administration fee will not be charged and the frequency of the account will revert back to its original status.
  • The New Due Date must be greater than 10 BUSINESS days from the previous payment on the old frequency.
  • An administration fee of $75.00 will be charged, the administration fee script must be read to the client.
